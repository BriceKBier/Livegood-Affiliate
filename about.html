The story behind the site

A practical life has taught me to value practical answers

I was born in New York City, spent part of my childhood in Hong Kong and later made Pittsburgh home for many years. My work has included technical laboratory experience, business, driving and nine years in real estate. Each chapter taught me something different about people, problem-solving and adapting when life changes.

After moving to the Myrtle Beach area, I spent nine years practicing real estate. I am no longer actively practicing, but I remain licensed in South Carolina with Innovate Real Estate and still welcome referral opportunities.

Why UCanWorkFromHome exists

I saw too much online information aimed at older adults falling into one of two categories: vague inspiration or exaggerated sales claims. Neither was very useful.

This site is my attempt to build something better—a trustworthy place where adults 50+ can explore work-from-home ideas, healthy-aging topics, wellness products and retirement lifestyle choices in plain English.

“My goal is to help readers make smarter decisions about their health, time and money—without pressure.”

What experience shapes my point of view

Technical work

Years in laboratory and coatings work taught me to look closely at ingredients, claims, details and evidence.

Real estate

Nine years of helping people make major decisions reinforced the importance of clarity, trust and honest tradeoffs. Although I no longer actively practice, I remain licensed in South Carolina with Innovate Real Estate and accept referrals.

Online business

Building websites and affiliate projects showed me both the genuine possibilities and the hype surrounding home-based income.

Life after 60

I am navigating the same questions my readers face: health, purpose, family, money and how to keep moving forward.

My editorial promise

  • I will explain things in plain English.
  • I will discuss drawbacks and costs—not just benefits.
  • I will not promise guaranteed income or health outcomes.
  • I will clearly disclose affiliate relationships.
  • I will keep the reader’s decision—not a sale—at the center.

Welcome

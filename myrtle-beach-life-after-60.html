Coastal living after 60

Is the Myrtle Beach area a good fit for your next chapter?

A practical, personal look at everyday life near the South Carolina coast—beyond the vacation brochures.

Living near Myrtle Beach can offer sunshine, recreation and a relaxed pace, but a successful move depends on matching the area to your budget, health needs and preferred lifestyle.

What many adults enjoy

Everyday advantages

  • Easy access to beaches, walking areas and outdoor activities.
  • A large population of retirees and newcomers, which can make meeting people easier.
  • Many neighborhoods and communities with different levels of activity and maintenance.
  • Plenty of restaurants, entertainment and seasonal events.

Tradeoffs to consider

  • Summer heat, humidity and heavy tourist traffic.
  • Storm preparation and insurance questions near the coast.
  • Rapid growth that can change traffic and development patterns.
  • Healthcare access can vary depending on specialty and location.

Think in terms of daily life—not vacation life

A week at the beach and a year at the beach are different experiences. Before moving, picture ordinary errands, medical appointments, grocery shopping, traffic, home maintenance and where you would build friendships.

Brice’s perspective: The best neighborhood is not automatically the one closest to the ocean. Convenience, flood exposure, traffic, maintenance and community fit may matter more over time.

Questions to ask before choosing an area

  1. How close do I need to be to healthcare?Map your likely doctors, hospital and pharmacy—not just the beach.
  2. Do I want activity or quiet?Some communities feel social and busy; others offer more space and privacy.
  3. What costs are easy to overlook?Consider insurance, HOA fees, storm preparation, taxes and maintenance.
  4. Will I enjoy the area year-round?Visit outside peak vacation season and test normal routines.

A sensible way to explore

Rent first when possible, visit several neighborhoods at different times of day and speak with residents about the parts of daily life that brochures do not mention.

Coastal South Carolina can be a wonderful next chapter. The key is choosing it for the life you will actually live—not only the view you enjoyed on vacation.

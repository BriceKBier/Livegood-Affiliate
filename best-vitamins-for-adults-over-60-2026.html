Healthy aging

Wellness products I personally use and consider

A personal, practical look at several wellness products for adults over 60, with an emphasis on informed choices.

Important: Supplements are personal. Review labels and discuss them with your physician or pharmacist, especially when you take prescription medication.

The products below are ones I use or have considered based on ingredients, convenience and value. They are not substitutes for a balanced diet, exercise, sleep or medical care.

Ultra Magnesium Complex

Magnesium supports many normal body functions, including muscle and nerve function. I use a multi-form magnesium product for convenience, but the right form and dose can vary from person to person.

Super Greens

A greens powder can be an easy way to add plant-based ingredients on busy days. I view it as a supplement to healthy food—not a replacement for vegetables and fruit.

Organic Coffee

Coffee can be part of an enjoyable daily routine. An organic option may appeal to people who want a simple ingredient list and a product they can compare on quality and price.

What matters more than the label

  • Check the ingredient amounts, not just the marketing language.
  • Consider possible medication interactions.
  • Introduce only one new supplement at a time.
  • Stop and seek medical advice if you notice an adverse effect.

Good health is built mostly through consistent habits. Supplements may play a supporting role when they fit your individual needs.

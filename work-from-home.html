Begin with the right question

Do you want a job, a service business or a long-term online asset?

Those are three very different paths. Knowing which one you want prevents wasted time and expensive detours.

A remote job may offer predictable pay. Freelancing gives more control but requires finding clients. Content, affiliate marketing and online selling can grow over time, but usually start slowly.

Brice’s rule of thumb

Start with what you already know

  1. List skills people have paid you for.
  2. Choose how many hours you truly want to work.
  3. Set a small startup budget.
  4. Test one path before buying courses or tools.

Protect yourself

Five warning signs of a bad opportunity

  • Guaranteed income or “risk-free” earnings.
  • Pressure to pay immediately.
  • Vague explanations of the actual work.
  • Income claims without typical-results context.
  • More emphasis on recruiting than delivering value.
A sensible first step

Choose one path and test it small

You do not need the perfect plan. You need a low-risk experiment that tells you whether the work suits your skills and your life.
